Learning Objectives

Learning objectives are the expressed goals for a lesson. They tell the student what they will be able to do, and how they will know they got there.

Write Learning Objectives to:

  • Plan for appropriate assessment and measurement
  • Give learners a means of organizing learning
  • Give instructors better perspective of learners.

Use student facing language to keep the focus on their outcomes. A common format is: “Students will be able to ____.”

Word choice establishes the level of cognition, so use a variety of critical thinking power verbs:

  • demonstrate
  • summarize
  • apply
  • organize
  • analyze
  • interpret
  • measure
  • adapt
  • create

Effective Learning Objectives are:

  • Attainable within the lesson time
  • Measurable
  • Specific
  • A clear fit with the big course goals
  • Framed in terms of “students will be able to...”
  • Visible to students during lessons
  • Referenced throughout lessons
